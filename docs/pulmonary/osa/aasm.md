# Summary

This document is the Bylaws of the American Academy of Sleep Medicine (updated January 2025), governing the corporate structure of the AASM as a Minnesota nonprofit. It is an organizational/governance document — not a clinical practice guideline — and contains no clinical recommendations, drug dosing, or diagnostic thresholds for sleep disorders. Content covers membership categories, Board of Directors composition and elections, Officer roles, and committee structure.

# Key Recommendations

- This source is an organizational bylaws document, not a clinical practice guideline — it contains no patient-care recommendations.
- Full AASM membership requires an MD, DO, PhD, DDS, DNP, or other healthcare doctoral degree with active involvement in sleep medicine.
- Members are automatically expelled after two months of dues delinquency.
- The Board of Directors comprises the President, President-Elect, Secretary/Treasurer, Immediate Past President, and eight Directors.
- Directors serve three-year terms, eligible for one additional consecutive term.
- President, President-Elect, and Immediate Past President each serve one-year terms; Secretary/Treasurer serves a three-year term.
- A majority of Directors constitutes a quorum; proxy voting is not permitted.
- Bylaw amendments require 60 days advance notice to membership plus a two-thirds vote of the Board of Directors.

# Thresholds & Doses

- 2 months dues delinquency — triggers automatic expulsion from membership
- 8 elected Directors — Board composition beyond the four Officers
- 3-year term — standard Director and Secretary/Treasurer term length
- 1-year term — President, President-Elect, and Immediate Past President
- 30 days — minimum interval ballots distributed before Annual Membership Meeting
- 20% write-in vote threshold — triggers a run-off election
- 60 days advance notice + 2/3 Board vote — required to amend Bylaws
- 3 at-large members on Leadership Development Committee, staggered 3-year terms, not eligible for re-election
